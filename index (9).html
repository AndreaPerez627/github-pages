Anthroplogy and Sociology

I am a motivated college student majoring in both sociology and anthropology, driven by a passion for understanding human behavior, cultural diversity, and societal structures. My academic journey has provided me with a strong foundation in critical thinking, research, and analysis, which I am eager to apply to real-world opportunities. While still early in my professional journey, I’ve gained valuable experience through babysitting, where I’ve developed important skills like responsibility, patience, problem-solving, and time management. Balancing multiple tasks and ensuring the well-being of children has helped me cultivate organizational skills and adaptability, which are essential in any career path.

Through my studies in sociology and anthropology, I’ve gained deep insights into social issues such as inequality, race, class, and gender, and how cultures shape human behavior. I am particularly interested in how societal structures impact individuals' lives, and I aim to use my knowledge to contribute to social change. By analyzing social systems and understanding cultural contexts, I believe I can make meaningful contributions to building a more just and equitable society. My involvement in Mexican culture and activism has further broadened my perspective, reinforcing my passion for fostering empathy and social change.

Outside of academics, I am actively seeking hands-on experience to further develop my skills through internships, volunteer work, or other opportunities. I’m eager to explore internships related to social research, cultural studies, or community development to apply my academic knowledge in a practical setting. With a commitment to both personal and professional growth, I am excited to continue expanding my knowledge and building a career that allows me to make a positive impact. I’m passionate about using my background in sociology and anthropology to address social issues and promote equality in meaningful ways.
